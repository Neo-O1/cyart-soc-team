# Incident Response Report 
## 1. Executive Summary

- **Incident ID:**  
- **Date/Time Detected:**  
- **Reported By / Detected By:** (Wazuh rule, user report, etc.)  
- **Short Summary:** (2–3 sentences, non‑technical where possible)  
- **Impact Summary:** (systems affected, data at risk, business impact level)

## 2. Scope

- Affected systems (hostnames, IPs)
- Affected users / accounts
- Time window of incident activity
- Related alerts / tickets

## 3. Timeline of Events

| Timestamp | Event / Action | Details |
|----------|----------------|---------|
|          |                |         |

## 4. Technical Details

- Initial detection: alert name, rule, log source
- Observed activities (IOC list: IPs, hashes, domains, URLs)
- MITRE ATT&CK mapping (tactics and techniques)
- Screenshots or references to dashboards

## 5. Impact Analysis

- Data affected (if any)
- Service impact (downtime, performance issues)
- Users impacted
- Estimated business risk (High / Medium / Low)

## 6. Containment

- Short‑term containment actions
- Long‑term containment (if any)
- Verification that malicious activity stopped

## 7. Eradication

- What was removed (malware, accounts, backdoors)
- Patches or configuration changes applied
- Validation steps (rescans, log reviews)

## 8. Recovery

- Systems restored (from backup or cleaned)
- Services brought back online
- Monitoring put in place post‑recovery

## 9. Lessons Learned

- What worked well
- What did not work well
- Detection or process gaps

## 10. Recommendations

- Technical: new rules, hardening, extra logging
- Process: updated playbooks/runbooks
- People: training or awareness
